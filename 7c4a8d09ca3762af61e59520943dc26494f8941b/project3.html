Wireframes & Prototyping We believe if we categorize default lists of words according to Vanessa's needs, she will save free time by not learning words she might not use in the future.

We believe if we develop an easy system to manage her study time in the app depending on her life’s commitments she won't miss a single day without learning or reviewing words.

We believe if we design a feature that allows her to highlight or categorize words in a different list of topics, she wouldn't spend a lot of time searching for the words she is interested to learn. Smart VocabularyIs an app for a language school that allows users to learn useful words in their life, no matter if they are traveling, working, or going to school.The main goal of the project was to successfully apply the design thinking process to a project to create a researched and tested set of mobile app designs as well as make end-users happy Smart Vocabulary focuses its features on personalization so users can choose how, when, where, and what to learn Objective
User research interviews User Personas Affinity mapping Task analysis and user flows Wireframing and prototyping Usability testing

Competitive analysis Vocabulary BuilderWordUp VocabularyFlashcards makerFor the first step, I identified three mobile apps that focus on learning new vocabulary. Parting from that, I got a better understanding of the problem and what kind of solutions have been already proposed to tackle the same problem as I did. For the competitive analysis, I wrote down the positive and negative aspects of each app by focusing on how easy the apps are to use and if they accomplish their up goals.
Pros : The progression of the difficulty levels can be easily seen at one glance and while advancing on the app, more words and levels are unlocked. This app also encourages users by praising them when finishing a level. Cons : There are no onboarding screens and the learning method is monotone and boring because it only has one kind of exercise. Pros : It has a personalized approach to learning and shows an overall progression of the user. Each word has a definition, synonyms, and examples. One can set reminders and goals that help users stay on track. Cons : Its examples as just extracts of videos and songs, meaning that visual examples would be a good addition. Reminders are just focused on daily routines but don't consider location or weekends. Pros: It has a personalized study plan and easy navigation through the categories. It has a step-by-step tutorial that shows the main features of the app. After realizing in some words, there are short games to reinforce what has been learned. Cons : Its words only include a translation. However, examples, images, and synonyms must be filled in by the user. If a mistake was saved while editing a word, there is no ‘’reset to default’’ button to recover the original information.Inspiration

    I enjoy testing the prototypes because lets me observe through their points of view and understand the product with real user interaction.

    Now I am more conscious of the perspective that nothing is fixed and everything can be improved if I embrace the process.

    The idea that users are the center of every design decision strengthened my passion for developing great products for people.
